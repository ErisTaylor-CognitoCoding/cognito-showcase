## The Brief I Gave

```
Lock the world rule first: everything lives inside a computer.
That's the north star every prompt feeds off.

Palette + mood — circuit-board greens, warning reds for infected
lanes, cool blues for clean data.

Towers: firewall (a brick energy barrier), antivirus (a rotating
scanner beam), packet-scanner (a radar dish).

Enemies: virus, worm, trojan — each visually distinct so a player
reads the threat at a glance.

The board: data-lanes drawn as glowing circuit traces the enemies
travel down.
```

## What the AI Built

Replit Agent produced a full visual language — not just individual assets, but a shared palette, tower sprites, enemy art and board design that held together as one thing. The firewall, antivirus scanner and packet-scanner each read instantly as defences. The virus, worm and trojan each read as different threat types. The circuit-trace data-lanes tied the board together. The key insight: brief the *world* first, not the *art* — and the AI makes design decisions that stay consistent across every asset that follows.

---

## Key Prompting Lessons

- Brief the **world rule** before asking for art — "this game lives inside a computer, towers are firewalls" gives the AI a theme to anchor every design decision to.
- Ask for a **colour palette first** — shared colours make every sprite look like it belongs in the same game.
- Build one asset, review it, then **reference it explicitly** in every prompt after: "match the style of that firewall tower."
- When something looks wrong, describe **why in plain words** — "too friendly-looking, make it feel more threatening" beats "try again."
- **Save every version** — AI won't remember last week's design, but paste a previous asset back in and it can anchor the next one.
