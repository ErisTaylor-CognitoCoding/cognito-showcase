## The Prompt I Gave

Here's roughly what I asked for:

```
A family phrasebook app for six languages — English, Bambara, Pashto, Kurdish Sorani,
Arabic and Albanian. A hundred essential phrases each, sorted into everyday categories.
Native script, English, a pronunciation guide and a real example sentence for every one.
Flashcards to practice, a translator between any two, and audio on everything.
Make it feel warm, like it belongs to a family, not a banking app.
```

One paragraph. Describing my family.

---

## What the AI Built

Family Lingo is a Progressive Web App with 500+ phrases across six languages — greetings, food, health, family, numbers, feelings, and more. Every phrase ships with the native script (Arabic, Pashto and Kurdish Sorani reading right-to-left, done properly), a pronunciation guide for people who need training wheels, and a real example sentence showing how a human would actually say the word. Flashcard mode for practice, an AI translator between any pair of the six languages, and audio on everything — tap a button and the phrase is read back to you. The seeding script died partway through more than once (marathon jobs in a shell that walks away — long story), so the fix was making the whole thing resumable, picking up exactly where it left off. The result installs on a phone like a proper app: add to home screen, terracotta icon, works offline on anything already loaded.
