## The Analogy

A playground see-saw — put 5 on one side, 10 on the other. The heavy side tips down. That's your program deciding which number is bigger.

Coding has special symbols for this. We call them **comparison operators**: greater than (`>`), less than (`<`), or are they equal (`==`).

---

## What It Does

```python
score = 8
if score > 5:
    print("You passed!")
```

See that `>` symbol? That's the see-saw. The program asks: is `score` bigger than `5`? Yes — so it prints "You passed!"

Flip it the other way and you get `<` (less than). Two equals signs (`==`) means both sides balance exactly — they're the same value.

One symbol. One question. One answer: bigger, smaller, or the same.
