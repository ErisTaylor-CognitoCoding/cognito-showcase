## POSITIONING CONTEXT

Government proposes four-tier SEND model (consultation closes 18 May 2026; legislation no earlier than Sept 2029).
- **Universal** — mainstream, no plan
- **Targeted** — Individual Support Plans (ISPs) replacing EHCPs for many pupils
- **Targeted Plus** — ISP + experts at hand + time-limited AP
- **Specialist** — EHCPs retained for most complex needs

**Key sales angle**: £1.6bn Inclusive Mainstream Fund paid DIRECT to schools in 2026/27 — bypassing LA procurement.
**Rules**: Do NOT claim regulatory urgency. Do NOT pitch LA frameworks. Pitch budget availability + "be ready" angle.

### Why now?

The SEND White Paper 2026 proposes routing £1.6bn in new Inclusive Mainstream Funding direct to schools — bypassing LAs entirely. No framework to wait for. No procurement panel to pitch to.

That means mainstream schools now have discretionary budget to commission AP-style provision — including accredited programmes — directly. Award Tracker is the tool that evidences it.

The AQA Unit Award Scheme itself is unaffected by the reform. Your accreditation pathway stays the same. What changes is who's buying.

**Current law stays in force until 2029.** There's no compliance deadline. But schools building Targeted Plus provision now will be the ones with clean, evidenced portfolios when Ofsted visits.
