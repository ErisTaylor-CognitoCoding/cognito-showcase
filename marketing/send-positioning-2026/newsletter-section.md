## What the SEND White Paper means for schools right now

The government published *Every Child Achieving and Thriving* in early 2026 — the biggest proposed overhaul of SEND support in a decade. Here's what it actually changes, what it doesn't, and what it means for your budget this year.

---

### What's being proposed

The White Paper sets out a four-tier model to replace the current binary EHCP / no-plan system:

- **Universal** — standard mainstream provision with no formal plan
- **Targeted** — Individual Support Plans (ISPs), a lighter document replacing EHCPs for many pupils
- **Targeted Plus** — ISP with specialist "experts at hand" input and time-limited alternative provision
- **Specialist** — EHCPs retained for pupils with the most complex needs

Alongside the new tiers, the government has committed:

- **£1.6bn** in an Inclusive Mainstream Fund, paid *directly to schools* in 2026/27
- **£1.8bn** for an "experts at hand" specialist service into mainstream settings

The consultation closes **18 May 2026**.

---

### What it doesn't mean

No compliance deadline. Current law stays in force until September 2029 at the earliest. You don't need to update your paperwork, change your EHCP processes, or panic.

The White Paper is a proposal. Legislation takes time. What arrives in 2029 may look different from what was consulted on in 2026.

---

### What it does mean — for your budget this year

The £1.6bn Inclusive Mainstream Fund is the real news, and it matters right now.

This money goes *direct to schools* — not via LAs, not through a commissioning framework, not after a 12-month procurement process. Headteachers and SENCOs have discretionary inclusion budget they can deploy on tools, provision, and support today.

If you've ever had a great inclusion tool stuck behind an LA procurement gate, that gate is getting smaller. The schools that get their tooling in place now will be the ones with clean evidence when the new system arrives.
